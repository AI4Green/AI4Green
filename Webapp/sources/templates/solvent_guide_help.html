Notes:

✝There are four CHEM21 hazard rating categories: Recommended, Problematic, Hazardous, and Highly Hazardous. Default ranking was determined from the Safety, Health, and Environmental scores. These ratings were updated after discussion leading to the fourth Highly Hazardous category. Cards are coloured to this overall rating. In this example, the overall rating of Hazardous is shown by the red border.

#CAS codes were collected from PubChem. The CAS code is linked to the corresponding page on PubChem.

*This data was provided by CHEM21.[1] See the CHEM21 publications for details on how individual Safety, Health, and Environment ratings were calculated. The radar plot also shows this data. The worst H3xx and H4xx are shown. Be aware that there may be other hazards associated with the solvent.

^This data is from an industry substitution guide.[2] This is purely to illustrate a potential issue with a solvent and how an alternative solvent may be more appropriate.
