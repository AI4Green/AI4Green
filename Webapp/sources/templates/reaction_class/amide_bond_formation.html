Amide Bond Formation

Amide bond formation is a condensation reaction between carboxylic acids and amines. Typically, a coupling agent and base are required.





This class of reaction can be carried out in a range of solvents including dipolar aprotic and chlorinated solvents. Important solvent properties are highly dependent on the process. For example, in solid phase peptide synthesis the solvent must be capable of swelling the resin and dissolving reactants and coupling reagents.
