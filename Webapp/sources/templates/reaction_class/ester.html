Ester Hydrolysis

Ester hydrolysis can be facilitated under basic or acidic conditions, breaking the ester bond and forming the corresponding carboxylic acid and alcohol as products. It is a widely used reaction and so there are many reported procedures in the literature.





A range of solvents can be used for ester hydrolysis. Though polar protic solvents are often chosen1 for their favourable solubilising effects, other reports2 have proven that the rate of hydrolysis can be enhanced by solvents with lower dielectric constants. To capture these effects, the full range of solvent descriptors was employed to build the PCA map.
