Suzuki-Miyaura coupling

The Suzuki-Miyaura reaction is a type of transition metal-catalysed cross-coupling that forms a carbon-carbon bond between a boronic ester and an aryl halide. Productivity in these reactions often depends on the choice of pre-catalyst, ligand, base, solvent, temperature and additives.





Suzuki-Miyaura reactions can be run in a wide range of solvents. Dipolar aprotics are the dominant choice due to their ability to dissolve lipophilic and organometalic compounds alike. However, a study conducted by Sherwood,1 demonstrated that solvent effects depend heavily on the specific conditions used, making it difficult to understand which solvent properties are important for high conversion. To capture this, a range of descriptors were used to generate the PCA map.

Experimental Data

Pd-catalysed Suzuki-Miyaura Coupling
A study by Lei et al.2 investigated the use of green solvents for the Suzuki-Miyaura coupling of Boc-protected amides with aryl boronic acids. The yield data for this investigation can be viewed by selecting "Suzuki-Miyaura Pd" in the Change Colour dropdown.


Ni-catalysed Suzuki-Miyaura Coupling
A separate investigation by Ramgren et al.3 undertook a solvent screen for the optimisation of a Ni-catalysed Suzuki-Miyaura cross coupling of napthyl sulphamate with phenyl bornic acid. The yield data for this investigation can be viewed by selecting "Suzuki-Miyaura Ni" in the Change Colour dropdown.
