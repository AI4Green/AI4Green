Alcohol Oxidation

Alcohols are usually oxidised to aldehydes or ketones using an oxidising agent. The nature of this oxidising agent varies greatly between reactions.





Due to the diverse nature of these processes, it is difficult to identify any prevalent solvent effects for the general reaction class. To encapsulate as much information as possible, all descriptors were selected as inputs for the PCA map.

Radical Mediated oxidation
A study by Janssen et al.,1 investigates the use of green solvents for the radical mediated oxidation of primary alcohols. No correlation between solvent properties and productivity was observed.
