Alkene Metathesis

Alkene Metathesis is a reaction that leads to transfer of substituents between two alkenes. This reaction can be reliably facilitated using the Hoveyda-Grubbs catalyst, which proceeds via a dissociative pathway.1




Solvents with high nucleophilic character can poison the active catalyst and non-electrostatic interactions (such as dispersion) can stabilise the starting alkenes, both of which lead to a reduction in reactivity.2 To encapsulate these effects, H-bonding and dispersion descriptors were chosen for the PCA.
