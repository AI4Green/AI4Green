Buchwald-Hartwig

The Buchwald-Hartwig reaction is a type of transition metal-catalysed cross-coupling that forms a carbon-nitrogen bond between a non-tertiary amine and an aryl halide.





Nonpolar aprotic solvents are commonly chosen for Buchwald-Hartwig amination reactions, and catalyst performance has been observed to be inversely proportional to the dielectric constant in the heterogeneous Cu-catalysed Buchwald-Hartwig reaction.1 To capture this in the PCA map, the following descriptors were used.

Experimental Data

A study by Lei et al.2 investigated the use of green solvents for the Buchwald-Hartwig coupling of Boc-protected amides with aryl amines. The yield data for this investigation can be viewed by selecting "Buchwald-Hartwig" in the Change Colour dropdown.
