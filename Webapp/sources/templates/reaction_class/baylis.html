Baylis-Hillman

The Baylis-Hillman reaction forms a carbon-carbon bond between an electrophilic alkene and a carbonyl compound, usually in the presence of a catalytic nucleophile. Tertiary amines or phosphines can be used to catalyse these reactions.





Computational investigations1 into the mechanism of the Baylis-Hillman reaction have showed that polar protic solvents aid the formation of important reaction intermediates, though they increase the activation energy for proton transfer. Other studies have highlighted the benefit of using solvents with H-bonding character2 and high dielectric constants3 for increasing the rate of reaction. Experimental Data

A report by Sangon et al.4 investigated the effects of solvent on the DABCO-catalysed Baylis-Hillman reaction of 4-nitrobenzaldehyde and methylacrylate. This experimental data can be viewed by selecting "Baylis-Hillman" in the Colour Change dropdown.
